Shape

A justification of the VSEPR shape

Compound: CH4 (Methane)

Shape: Tetrahedron


Basic Overview:

The VSEPR theory states that atoms want to stay as far away from each other (because they're antisocial, which I understand), but at the same time they have to stay near the central atom (in this case, carbon) as much as possible. If you don't understand, I found a dude who looks like Jesus (Professor Dave) that explains it in a video (on the home page).

Bond angles:

Since there are 4 Hydrogen atoms surrounding one Carbon atom, the final shape would be a tetrahedron on the 3D plane. Since the charges of each H-C-H bond is equal to each other, the bond angles are a consistent 109.5 degrees.
